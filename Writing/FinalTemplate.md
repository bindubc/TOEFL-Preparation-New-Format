### **TOEFL Writing Templates for the Two Question Types**

Below are templates you can follow for each TOEFL Writing task: **Integrated Writing Task** and **Independent Writing Task**. These templates provide a structure you can adapt to various prompts.

---

### **1. Integrated Writing Task Template**
The Integrated Writing Task asks you to summarize the relationship between the information in the reading passage and the lecture. Typically, the lecture either supports or contradicts the reading.

#### **Template:**

**Introduction**  
- The reading passage and lecture discuss [topic].  
- While the reading states that [main idea of the reading], the lecture challenges this view by arguing [main idea of the lecture].  

**Body Paragraph 1**  
- According to the reading, [Point 1 from the reading].  
- However, the lecturer contradicts this by stating that [Point 1 from the lecture].  
- The lecturer further explains that [supporting detail from the lecture].  

**Body Paragraph 2**  
- The reading also claims that [Point 2 from the reading].  
- In contrast, the lecturer argues that [Point 2 from the lecture].  
- Specifically, the lecturer mentions [supporting detail from the lecture].  

**Body Paragraph 3**  
- Furthermore, the reading asserts that [Point 3 from the reading].  
- Conversely, the lecturer refutes this by stating [Point 3 from the lecture].  
- The lecture provides additional evidence that [supporting detail from the lecture].  

**Conclusion** (Optional)  
- In conclusion, the lecture clearly challenges the claims made in the reading by presenting [summary of lecture points].  

---

### **2. Independent Writing Task Template**
The Independent Writing Task requires you to present your opinion on a given topic and support it with reasons and examples.

#### **Template:**

**Introduction**  
- [Hook or general statement about the topic].  
- I strongly believe that [your opinion]. This is because [Reason 1] and [Reason 2].  

**Body Paragraph 1**  
- First of all, [Reason 1].  
- For example, [specific example or detail to support Reason 1].  
- This clearly shows that [explanation of how the example supports your opinion].  

**Body Paragraph 2**  
- Another important reason is [Reason 2].  
- To illustrate, [specific example or detail to support Reason 2].  
- Therefore, it is evident that [explanation of how the example supports your opinion].  

**Body Paragraph 3 (Optional)**  
- Some people might argue that [counterargument].  
- However, this is not true because [refutation of the counterargument].  
- As a result, [final explanation to reinforce your opinion].  

**Conclusion**  
- In conclusion, I strongly believe that [restate your opinion].  
- This is due to [Reason 1] and [Reason 2].  
- Overall, [final thought or recommendation].  

---

### **Tips for Using These Templates**
1. **Adapt to the Prompt:** Ensure you tailor the content of the template to the specific question being asked.  
2. **Use Transitions:** Add phrases like *"Moreover," "In addition," "However,"* and *"For example"* to improve flow.  
3. **Focus on Grammar and Clarity:** Use simple, clear sentences to minimize errors.  
4. **Stick to the Word Count:** Write 250–300 words for the Integrated Task and 300–350 words for the Independent Task.

These templates will help you structure your essays effectively and meet TOEFL expectations!
