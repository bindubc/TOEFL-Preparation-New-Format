
#### Quick Summary of Formats by Question Type:
* Question 1 (Independent): Point-Reason-Example (PRE)
* Question 2 (Campus Issue): Problem-Solution-Result (PSR)
* Question 3 (Academic Reading & Listening): Describe-Explain-Impact (DEI)
* Question 4 (Lecture): Compare-Contrast-Preference (CCP) or Why-What-How (WWH)

##### Format:
Best Format: Point-Reason-Example (PRE)

* 5-7 seconds (Point): In my opinion, [state your preference].
* 15-20 seconds (Reason):: The main reason is that [explain your reason].
* 15-20 seconds (Example): For example, [provide a specific example or scenario].
* 3-5 seconds (Restate): That’s why I believe [restate your point].

  #### FORMAT:
    Best Format Q3:  Integrated Reading and Listening Task  (Academic Topic =>  Describe-Explain-Impact (DEI)

      * Briefly describe the concept.
      * Explain the professor’s examples.
      * Highlight their significance or how they relate to the topic.

      Template (DEI):

      * Describe: The concept discussed is [briefly describe the concept].
      * Explain: The professor provides an example, such as [summarize the example].
      * Impact: This example illustrates [explain how the example relates to the concept].

    Best Format: Integrated Listening Task (Lecture) => Compare-Contrast-Preference (CCP) or Why-What-How (WWH)

     *  Use CCP if comparing two adaptations.
     *   Use WWH if explaining the mechanisms of adaptation.

     Option 1: Compare-Contrast-Preference (CCP)

* Compare: The professor discusses [topic] and compares two approaches: [Option 1] and [Option 2].
* Contrast: [Option 1] focuses on [specific details], while [Option 2] emphasizes [specific details].
* Preference (if applicable): I think [state preference] is better because [reason].

Option 2: Why-What-How (WWH)

* Why: The professor explains [why the topic is important or happens].
* What: He/she provides details, such as [examples or explanation].
* How: This helps to understand [impact or significance].
