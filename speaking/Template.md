Here are **templates** for the different tasks in the new TOEFL iBT Speaking section. These templates provide a clear structure to help you organize your responses effectively.

---

### **1. Independent Task Template (Familiar Topics)**  
This task asks for your opinion on a familiar topic.

#### **Template**  
1. **Introduction (State your opinion clearly)**  
   - "I strongly believe that [your opinion] because of two main reasons."  
   - "In my opinion, [your opinion]. There are two reasons for this."

2. **Reason 1 (Explain with details/examples)**  
   - "First, [state your first reason]. For example, [provide a specific example or personal experience]."  
   - "This is important because [explain why this reason matters]."

3. **Reason 2 (Explain with details/examples)**  
   - "Second, [state your second reason]. For instance, [give another example or explanation]."  
   - "This demonstrates that [connect back to your opinion]."

4. **Conclusion (Restate your opinion)**  
   - "In conclusion, [restate your opinion briefly]."  
   - "That's why I think [your opinion]."

---

### **2. Integrated Task 1 Template (Reading + Listening + Speaking)**  
You summarize and integrate information from a reading passage and a listening lecture.

#### **Template**  
1. **Introduction (Main idea of both sources)**  
   - "The reading passage explains that [main idea of the reading]. The lecture, however, challenges this by stating that [main idea of the lecture]."

2. **Point 1 (Key detail from the reading + opposing point from the lecture)**  
   - "According to the reading, [state a key point]. It suggests that [explain further]."  
   - "In contrast, the speaker argues that [state the opposing point from the lecture] and provides an example to support it."

3. **Point 2 (Another key detail from the reading + opposing point from the lecture)**  
   - "The reading also mentions that [state another point]."  
   - "On the other hand, the speaker counters this by saying [explain how the lecture opposes or modifies this idea]."

4. **Conclusion (Summarize the opposition)**  
   - "Overall, the lecture disagrees with the reading by providing examples that [summarize key points of disagreement]."

---

### **3. Integrated Task 2 Template (Listening + Speaking)**  
You summarize a conversation between two people about a campus-related issue.

#### **Template**  
1. **Introduction (State the issue and opinions)**  
   - "The conversation is about [state the issue]. The man/woman supports/opposes this idea because of two reasons."

2. **Reason 1 (First reason the speaker gives)**  
   - "First, the man/woman believes that [state the first reason]. For example, [provide details or example from the conversation]."

3. **Reason 2 (Second reason the speaker gives)**  
   - "Second, he/she mentions that [state the second reason]. This is important because [explain why it supports their opinion]."

4. **Conclusion (Restate their opinion)**  
   - "Therefore, the man/woman thinks that [summarize their opinion briefly]."

---

### **4. Integrated Task 3 Template (Listening + Speaking: Academic Lecture)**  
You summarize key points from an academic lecture.

#### **Template**  
1. **Introduction (Main idea of the lecture)**  
   - "The lecture discusses [main topic] and explains two/three main points about it."

2. **Point 1 (First key point)**  
   - "First, the speaker states that [explain the first key point]. For instance, [provide an example or explanation from the lecture]."

3. **Point 2 (Second key point)**  
   - "Second, he/she mentions that [explain the second key point]. This is significant because [explain why it matters]."

4. **Optional: Point 3 (Third key point, if applicable)**  
   - "Finally, the lecturer talks about [third key point]. For example, [add details from the lecture]."

5. **Conclusion (Summarize the lecture briefly)**  
   - "In summary, the lecture provides information about [main topic] by discussing [key points]."

---

### **Tips for Using These Templates**
- **Personalize** them with specific details from the task.
- Use **connectors** like "Moreover," "In addition," or "However" to make transitions smoother.
- Practice with sample questions to make these structures feel natural.

These templates will help you stay organized and convey your ideas confidently during the test!
