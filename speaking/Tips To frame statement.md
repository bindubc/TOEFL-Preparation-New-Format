 follow a structured format to organize your thoughts and keep your responses concise and clear. Below are several formats you can use depending on the type of TOEFL Speaking prompt:

---

### **1. Point-Reason-Example (PRE)**
**Best for:** Opinion or preference questions.  
**Steps:**  
1. **Point:** State your main idea or choice.  
2. **Reason:** Explain why you think this way.  
3. **Example:** Provide a specific example to support your reason.  

**Example Prompt:** *Do you prefer studying at home or in a library?*  
**Response:**  
*"I prefer studying at home. It’s quieter and more comfortable for me. For instance, at home, I can avoid distractions and take breaks whenever I need to. This helps me stay focused and be more productive."*

---

### **2. Problem-Solution-Result (PSR)**  
**Best for:** Problem-solving or decision-making questions.  
**Steps:**  
1. **Problem:** State the issue or challenge.  
2. **Solution:** Describe how to solve it.  
3. **Result:** Explain the benefit or outcome of your solution.  

**Example Prompt:** *Your school cafeteria serves unhealthy food. What would you suggest to improve it?*  
**Response:**  
*"The problem is that unhealthy food impacts students’ health. My solution is to introduce more nutritious options like salads and fruits. This would result in healthier students who perform better academically and feel more energetic."*

---

### **3. Describe-Explain-Impact (DEI)**  
**Best for:** Descriptive or experience-based questions.  
**Steps:**  
1. **Describe:** Mention the person, place, or event.  
2. **Explain:** Highlight its key qualities or characteristics.  
3. **Impact:** Share how it influenced or benefited you.  

**Example Prompt:** *Describe a memorable trip you’ve taken.*  
**Response:**  
*"Last year, I visited the mountains. The air was fresh, and the scenery was beautiful with snow-covered peaks. This trip was unforgettable because it gave me a chance to relax and enjoy nature."*

---

### **4. Agree/Disagree (A-R-E)**
**Best for:** Agree or disagree questions.  
**Steps:**  
1. **Agree/Disagree:** Clearly state your position.  
2. **Reason:** Provide a logical explanation.  
3. **Example:** Add an example or evidence to strengthen your argument.  

**Example Prompt:** *Do you agree that smartphones are more useful than laptops?*  
**Response:**  
*"I agree because smartphones are more portable and versatile. For example, I use mine to send emails, attend virtual meetings, and even edit documents while traveling. This makes it more convenient than a laptop."*

---

### **5. Compare-Contrast-Preference (CCP)**  
**Best for:** Comparison questions.  
**Steps:**  
1. **Compare:** Mention similarities between the two options.  
2. **Contrast:** Highlight the differences.  
3. **Preference:** State your choice and explain why.  

**Example Prompt:** *Compare studying with friends and studying alone. Which do you prefer?*  
**Response:**  
*"Studying with friends is interactive, while studying alone is more focused. I prefer studying alone because I can concentrate better and finish tasks faster. For example, I usually prepare for exams alone to avoid distractions."*

---

### **6. Why-What-How (WWH)**  
**Best for:** Questions asking for explanation or future plans.  
**Steps:**  
1. **Why:** State why something happens or matters.  
2. **What:** Describe what can be done or what happens as a result.  
3. **How:** Explain how it benefits or works.  

**Example Prompt:** *Why is learning a second language important?*  
**Response:**  
*"Learning a second language is important because it opens up opportunities. For example, it helps with traveling and communicating with people from different cultures. This builds connections and enhances career prospects."*

---

### **7. Opinion-Reason-Impact (ORI)**  
**Best for:** Expressing opinions or beliefs.  
**Steps:**  
1. **Opinion:** Share your viewpoint or belief.  
2. **Reason:** Provide a strong reason for your belief.  
3. **Impact:** Explain the effect or importance of your opinion.  

**Example Prompt:** *Do you think teamwork is more important than individual effort?*  
**Response:**  
*"I think teamwork is more important because it combines different skills and ideas. For example, in group projects, everyone contributes unique strengths. This often leads to better results than working alone."*
